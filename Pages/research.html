A Summation-Based Algorithm For Integer Factorization: A Quantum Approach


Justin Friedlander
May 2025
Integer factorization is a problem at the core of modern cryptography approaches such as RSA. As such, much effort has been put towards finding a fast factorization algorithm. However, no classical algorithm has been realized to efficiently factor two numbers. In 1994, Peter Shor discovered an algorithm that could efficiently factor integers using quantum computers. However, efforts still remain to make better classical or quantum factorization methods.

This paper recalls a classical approach which runs an integer factorization algorithm in \(O( \sqrt{n} )\) time. It then provides an outline for how to improve this time complexity to \(O( n^{\frac{1}{4}} )\) by making use of the Grover's Search algorithm on a quantum computer.

A Summation-Based Algorithm For Integer Factorization


Justin Friedlander
March 2025
Numerous methods have been considered to create a fast integer factorization algorithm. Despite its apparent simplicity, the difficulty to find such an algorithm plays a crucial role in modern cryptography, notably, in the security of RSA encryption. Some approaches to factoring integers quickly include the Trial Division method, Pollard's Rho and p-1 methods, and various Sieve algorithms.

This paper introduces a new method that converts an integer into a sum in base-2. By combining a base-10 and base-2 representation of the integer, an algorithm on the order of \(\sqrt{n}\) time complexity can convert that sum to a product of two integers, thus factoring the original number.
